The effects of switching to fossil fuels

This switch to fossil fuels had far-reaching effects on global carbon dioxide levels. The burning of these fuels releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, contributing to a marked increase in greenhouse gas concentrations. The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency notes that since the Industrial Revolution, human activities have been responsible for almost all of the increase in greenhouse gases in the atmosphere [1].

More specifically, the Global Carbon Project reported that global carbon emissions from fossil fuels have significantly increased since 1900. From less than 2 billion tons of carbon dioxide in 1900, the emissions have risen to over 36 billion tons in 2019 [2]. This trend is linked to both industrial activities and changes in land use (like deforestation), which decrease the earth's capacity to absorb CO2. Deforestation is particularly significant because trees absorb carbon dioxide, so fewer trees mean more CO2 stays in the atmosphere.
